
# ALLOCATE Capital 

A physical, transparent model for allocating capital across time.

Allocate Capital is the companion system to Allocate Energy.

Allocate Energy governs how energy is harvested, stored, consumed, and transformed.

Allocate Capital governs how financial capital enters, persists, decays, and is converted into physical capacity.

This is not an investment platform.

It is not a financial product.

It is a capital allocation interface grounded in physical reality.


---

## Overview

All physical systems require capital to exist.

In an Energy Economy solar panels must be purchased. 
Batteries must be installed. Controllers, sensors, enclosures, and labor must be paid for.

Allocate Capital provides a clear, measurable, and honest way for individuals and institutions to allocate fiat capital into physical energy nodes without abstraction, leverage, or fictional promises.

Capital enters once.

It becomes hardware.

From that point on, physics, not finance, determines outcomes.

## The Five Core Axioms of Capital Allocation

**Axiom 1 — Origin**

All capital originates from prior allocation.

Capital is not created from nothing.
Every unit of capital represents time, energy, risk, or labor previously allocated elsewhere. Capital is accumulated choice.


---

**Axiom 2 — Conversion**

Capital enters a physical system only through conversion.

Capital has no effect until it is converted into assets, tools, or infrastructure. Once converted, capital ceases to exist as capital and becomes physical reality.


---

**Axiom 3 — Sunkness**

Converted capital is irreversible.

Once capital becomes hardware, it cannot be unspent. Reversing a capital allocation requires additional capital and introduces loss. There is no undo in physical systems.


---

**Axiom 4 — Decay**

All physical expressions of capital decay.

Assets degrade through use, environment, and time. This decay is governed by physics, not accounting rules. Ignoring decay does not prevent it.


---

**Axiom 5 — Surplus**

Returns emerge only from constrained operation over time.

Capital does not produce returns by itself. Surplus arises only when physical systems operate within constraints and generate outputs exceeding their ongoing requirements. No surplus is guaranteed.
